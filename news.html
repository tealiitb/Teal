News & Updates

June 15, 2023
New Research Grant Awarded

Our lab has been awarded a prestigious grant to study climate change impacts on water resources in South Asia. This three-year project will focus on developing innovative remote sensing techniques to monitor water resources in changing climate scenarios.

The project aims to create new methodologies for assessing water availability and quality using thermal remote sensing data, with a particular focus on regions vulnerable to climate change impacts.

May 3, 2023
Paper Published in Remote Sensing Journal

Our recent work on thermal remote sensing applications has been published in the Remote Sensing journal. The paper, titled "Advanced Thermal Remote Sensing Techniques for Evapotranspiration Estimation," presents a novel approach to estimating evapotranspiration using multi-sensor satellite data.

This research demonstrates significant improvements in accuracy compared to traditional methods, especially in heterogeneous landscapes.

April 12, 2023
PhD Student Wins Best Presentation Award

Congratulations to our PhD student for winning the best presentation award at the International Remote Sensing Conference. The presentation, titled "Machine Learning Approaches for Downscaling Land Surface Temperature," showcased innovative techniques for improving the spatial resolution of thermal satellite data.

March 20, 2023
New Laboratory Equipment

We are pleased to announce the acquisition of new state-of-the-art thermal imaging equipment for our laboratory. This equipment will enhance our capabilities for field measurements and validation of satellite-derived thermal data.

February 5, 2023
Collaboration with International Research Institute

TEAL has established a new collaboration with the International Water Management Institute (IWMI) to work on joint research projects related to water resources management in agricultural regions.

January 10, 2023
Workshop on Remote Sensing Applications

Our lab successfully conducted a two-day workshop on "Remote Sensing Applications in Hydrology" for graduate students and early-career researchers. The workshop covered various aspects of using satellite data for hydrological applications.
